More Ridiculous Copyright Law

Looks like the industry lapdogs in Washington are preparing even more draconian law as a followup to the DMCA.  The DMCA, itself a horrible law, has turned a large percentage of Internet users into felons.  It gets even more surreal when the AG throws out this ridiculous claim to justify the new proposal:

Such changes are necessary because new technology is “encouraging large-scale criminal enterprises to get involved in intellectual-property theft,” Gonzales said, adding that proceeds from the illicit businesses are used, “quite frankly, to fund terrorism activities.”

That’s right.  Apparently the Mob and Al Queda want to sell you the latest Britney Spears album in order to fund their own nefarious schemes.

This is just another example of corporate-friendly, innovation-killing legislation that is slowing choking this country.  Thinking about  remixing old music, providing universal garage door openers or cheap printer ink refills?  Make sure you have a lawyer on retainer and plenty of cash to fight off the DMCA lawsuits from your bigger corporate competitors.  This law is just another kick to the gut of the average American consumer.
