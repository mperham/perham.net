New Bike Toys

Saturday, October 30th, 2004

It’s amazing how buying one thing will cause you to have to buy 2-3 other things. The costs just rack up… I currently have commuter tires on the SV, not really befitting it’s new primary purpose of track bike. So I put out a call for tire ideas from my friends and one offered his used track day takeoffs that still have a decent amount of tread. These tires are about $350 new and he sold them to me for $50. But most tire changing places require you to bring in the wheels so now I need to buy f/r stands for the bike to pull the wheels off myself. So I buy a set of Handy Stands to deal with the tires along with a set of spools to attach to the swingarm. At least the stands will make maintainence much easier to deal with.

Track Day

Tuesday, October 26th, 2004

Had a miserable track day at MSR yesterday. It rained the day before so it was damp and cloudy in the morning. On top of the foreboding weather, I had my suspension looked at by a pro and he said my SV’s rear shock is completely blown. Now I need to go buy an aftermarket shock ($6-800). I went out anyways (I’m not even fast enough to know when my bike’s shock sucks…) and found the bike to be squirmy around under me whenever I go over seams in the track where water is weeping out from. In total I got one fast session with a grand total of TWO fast laps (the other laps I was held up by slower riders) before lunch. Just after lunch right before we were to go out again it starts to rain… So we pack up and go home at 1pm. In the end I took a day off work and spent $115 in track fees plus $65 in expenses to get two fast laps on a broken bike. Argh.

Walmart for Motorcycles

Tuesday, October 12th, 2004

I went to Woods Fun Center today to get keys made for my SV650S. WFC is the local “megamart” for motorcycles - they carry all four Japanese brands and tons of apparel and parts. The problem is that they have “small boutique” prices (i.e. everything at MSRP) and a reputation for terrible customer service.

Anyhow I somehow lost the key in the past 48 hours and couldn’t find the backup but I had written the code for the key in my owners manual. So I tell the fellow at the parts counter my key code, Axxxx, and he proceeds to make me two copies. I get home and they DON’T WORK! How do you mess up a five character code??? Now I’ve got to go back there and get them replaced. The only good to come out of this was I found the backup key so I can show them what a “good” copy looks like and be sure I get one…

All this was done because I was trying to prep the SV for trackdays later this month. I’m going to be doing track days on 10/25 and 10/30 in Fort Worth and College Station respectively. Whoo hoo!

IBM, or, The Arch-Nemesis

Friday, October 8th, 2004

I received a rude shock this week when porting our new application from Resin to Websphere. The way we were doing asynchronous JMS messaging was completely forbidden in Websphere and requires a major rearchitecture of the application. As a side effect, we also need to use Websphere everywhere - not just in production but also in development on our own machines.

This wouldn’t be a problem if Websphere was good software. The problem is that it is not. It is absolutely terrible from a development perspective as it forces you to work the way it wants to, not the way you want to. I also never thought I’d say this but it is incredibly over-documented. Everything has 300 pages of documentation to go along with it but everything is so poorly indexed that you can’t find the one piece of documentation you want.

I’m wondering if 30 years from now I will be a Willy Loman-type figure, slouched over and beaten down by decades of abuse at the hands of the most evil software ever created. God help my soul.
