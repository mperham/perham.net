Spam Busting successful!

I have been using the internet for the past 10 years and during that time several of my email addresses have found their way onto the email lists of spammers. I usually get about 30-40 spam emails per day and in an effort to rid myself of them automatically I installed SpamAssassin a year ago. It always worked pretty well but recently I moved over to use my mail provider’s custom SpamAssassin installation. This move to a shared copy has made it work even better. The absolute key to getting it to work well is to use blocklists so that mail from known spamming servers get tagged as such by adding this to your ~/.spamassassin/user_prefs

score RCVD_IN_RBL 5.0
score RCVD_IN_RSS 5.0
score RCVD_IN_DUL 5.0
score RCVD_IN_BL_SPAMCOP_NET 5.0
score KOREAN_UCE_SUBJECT 5.0
score RCVD_IN_UNCONFIRMED_DSBL 3.0
score RCVD_IN_RFCI 3.0

Now I get about 1 spam per day in my inbox, about 5 in a Not Sure folder and about 35 automatically deleted. I’ve never actually seen a legitimate email in the Not Sure folder.
