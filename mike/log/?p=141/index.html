Video Woes

I’ve been getting video artifacts displayed occasionally on my flat panel monitor for the last few days. I bought a small form factor (SFF) machine a few weeks ago to replace my super-loud desktop. The only problem was that the SFF’s on-board video only has a VGA connection and the resulting video quality on my flat panel is dismal compared to the DVI connection. Well I figured would use the video card I had in the desktop but that video card has a loud fan on it. So to get blissful silence AND acceptable video quality I unplugged the video card’s fan and also underclocked it so (hopefully) it would run cool enough with just the heat sink.

So when I started getting video anomolies the other day, my first thought was “Well, the jig is up old boy”. I guess you can run but you can’t hide from the Laws of Thermodynamics. Anyhow I bought a new, passively-cooled video card from Frys yesterday and put it in. The video is more stable but I’m still seeing some wierdness. I’m hoping my three year old flat panel is not giving up the ghost here… I can deal with a new $70 video card but having to get a new $700 monitor would suck.
