August 27, 2003

In living color

I got the HDTV box Monday afternoon and had quite a time trying to get all the local stations on my cheap antenna. I already had a cable run from the attic since I had envisioned having an HD antenna a few months ago when I was wiring my system. I bought a little dohickey to link the antenna and the cable and hooked that up to the box and WHAMMO! I now have all three HD stations broadcasting in Austin.

It was a pain in the butt to set up the wiring for the components though. My receiver only has two digital optical inputs and I have three optical components: Tivo, DVD and HDTV box. Thus my DVD player has been rendered mute. I mainly use it for playing CDs and I have a little MP3 CD player also hooked up so it shouldn't be that big of a deal. Maybe a year from now I'll pick up an HD Tivo (with DVD built in?) which will consolidate much of this mess into a neater package.

So the question on everyone's minds: how's the HD quality? Pretty good - it's a very noticable improvement over standard definition. Becky and I did an A/B test with Leno last night and her comment? "You can see the color of his eyes!" 'Nuff said.

New Toys

It's been months since I bought any toys for myself. I've been dying to upgrade my 3-yr old Nokia 8260 cell phone and buy a high definition decoder so I can take advantage of my home theatre system. It just so happened that I bought both of them Wednesday. I bought a Sony Ericsson T616 phone through AT&T Wireless and a used Samsung SIR-T151 through eBay.

The phone has a camera built into it, a huge color screen but is still the same size as my 8260 - amazing! So far, so good. UPS still has the HiDef box - I'll report more when I get it hooked up.
