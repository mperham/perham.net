Tivo upgrade time!

I bought a 120GB drive last night and started hacking my tivo. First I copied the stock 40GB drive onto the new 120GB drive. More difficult though was the process of getting network access. Out of the box, the Hughes HDVR2 does not support Ethernet connectivity - you are forced to use a phone line and modem. This is despite the fact that the platform it is based on, the Tivo Series 2, DOES support ethernet connections. Apparently though you can hack the software to use ethernet - all the code is there, they just don't load the network drivers. Anyhow I tried to load the drivers last night but had no luck. I'm going to try again now and see if I can't get it right this time around.

eBay sucks

I've been trying to sell my laptop on ebay for the last 2 weeks because it's just too big and heavy for couch surfing. In general my impression is that there are a lot of creeps and frauds out there. These morons have several tricks up their sleeve:

1) Lowball you in hopes that you have no idea what your item is worth. My laptop cost me $2500 4 months ago and would cost about $2100 new from Dell today. I've had people offer me $850 (?!) and $1000 for it. Gee thanks.

2) Ask your reserve price. There's a reason why ebay doesn't show this to bidders! Do you often walk into a store and ask the salesman "What's the store's cost on this item?"

3) Entice you to ship it to some Eastern European/Asian country with promises of a great price. You'd be surprised how many US businessmen are travelling in Russia, want me to ship my laptop to them to replace their broken machine and will pay $1000 above my reserve as soon as they get it for my trouble.

If it doesn't sell tomorrow I'll probably just forget the whole thing and either sell it locally or live with the machine despite it's size and weight.
