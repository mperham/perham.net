Track Day

Well, this track day was a little more exciting than the usual track day. I reserved a U-Haul motorcycle trailer Saturday morning for pickup the next day. When I got there, the fellow checked the lights only to find that the right blinker did not work. 30 minutes of rewiring proved fruitless and I left with nothing but consolation from the gentleman.

So Plan B - Mom and Dad would drive down to College Station with me riding along in caravan Sunday night. I would stay at their hotel room that night, ride in the track day, stay the next night and ride home at the crack of dawn Tuesday morning.

My goal for this track day was to get down to a 2:15 lap time. I was turning 2:25 last time I was there 4-5 track days ago so I figured a 10 second drop would not be out of the question. My first session saw a 2:22 and I knew the times would drop over the course of the day. Group C (my group, the slow group) proved to be slow but sparse enough where traffic was rarely an issue. I passed almost everyone at will and was only passed once by a psycho on a red SV who almost lost the rear end as he passed me on the inside. I followed him with no small amount of rage just to prove I was as fast as him and wound up turning my fastest lap of the day: 2:16. He got away from me in traffic but I stayed with him for the half lap before.

There was a dude on a Harley V-Rod cruising around - that was pretty cool. He was very slow (I passed him twice per session usually) but I was glad he was out there if only for the entertainment value!

I managed to find a ride home that night with Robin, a friend from my motorcycle board. She was taking another guy from the board, Byron. Unfortunately Byron drew the short straw, crashed in the afternoon and got a sweet ride to the hospital for his trouble. We packed up one session early in order to pick him up from the hospital. He was unharmed except for a concussion which played havok with his short term memory. Is there anything funnier/more tiring than a person asking you the same question every 5 minutes?

He’s talking about selling his bike and picking up a metric cruiser instead. I think that’s an overreaction - hopefully he will let a few weeks pass to get some perspective and avoid a rash decision.

Anywho, I think I’ll be upgrading to Group B next trackday. All my friends in Group B seem to think I’ll be plenty fast to hang with the midpack. It’s about time!
