Electronic Notes

I stopped by Frys this morning for my quarterly pilgrimage to Electronics Mecca. I wanted to possibly buy a Game Boy Micro, maybe a clipart package (I need some good looking icons and graphics for my top secret project and I’m having a hard time finding free stuff on the web) and check out the ipod nano and anything else that struck my fancy. Some notes:

  • The G5 PowerMacs are much faster than my Mac mini. Anyone want to donate $2500 to my PowerMac fund?
  • The ipod nano is tiny. Seriously tiny. Too tiny IMO. It’s almost like a Shuffle with a screen. The scroll wheel felt a little flimsy and was too small to use comfortably with my admittedly large hands.
  • The Gameboy Micro is not available until later this week. Dum kopf.
  • Every clipart package requires Windows. Not surprising but disappointing.
  • Frys has gotten much better about showing off their TVs. They had all of the high-end displays playing HDNet, specifically they were playing a news clip with scenes from New Orleans and the destruction really hit home because of the HD quality. Now I want a flat panel display with HDNet.
  • JVC is the first camcorder vendor to actually “get it” and come out with a camcorder that records MPEG-2 directly to an internal hard drive. Video can be edited on the PC/Mac and burned directly to a DVD without loss of quality. Kudos. Now I just need to wait for the second-gen product for the kinks to be ironed out and the price to come down a few hundred bucks. Actually I want one that does HD and will record to HD-DVD.
