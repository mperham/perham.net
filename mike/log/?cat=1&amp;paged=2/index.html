It’s official: Bush is a Dictactor

Sunday, January 15th, 2006

This according to Bruce Schneier:

The result is that the president’s wartime powers, with its armies, battles, victories, and congressional declarations, now extend to the rhetorical “War on Terror”: a war with no fronts, no boundaries, no opposing army, and — most ominously — no knowable “victory.” Investigations, arrests and trials are not tools of war. But according to the Yoo memo, the president can define war however he chooses, and remain “at war” for as long as he chooses.

This is precisely what I was getting at when I blogged about the nonsensical “War on Terror”.

This is indefinite dictatorial power. And I don’t use that term lightly; the very definition of a dictatorship is a system that puts a ruler above the law. In the weeks after 9/11, while America and the world were grieving, Bush built a legal rationale for a dictatorship. Then he immediately started using it to avoid the law.

Rats!

Monday, December 12th, 2005

I have RATS in my walls!!! I don’t know how they got there but last night I could clearly hear them moving around in the wall between the guest bedroom and bathroom. I thought they had chewed through the walls and were in the bathroom, which got my rat-killin’ ire up. They are nasty disease carriers though so I didn’t want to have to face them mano-a-rato. I decided to send in a proxy: I cracked the door, threw in Molly Cat and slammed it shut. She’s killed birds before so I know she’s got the instinct of a rat-killer. Anyhow I heard nothing from her for a few seconds so I opened the door - nothing. But you could definitely hear them so now I need to figure out how they got into the house and how to kill them.

Death to Rats!
